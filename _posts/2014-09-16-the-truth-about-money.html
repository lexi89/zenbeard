--- title: Good or Bad? - The Truth About Money date: 2014-09-16 03:15:26.000000000 +02:00 categories: life-design ---

People tend to fall in one of two camps: “money is good” or “money is bad”.

The "money is bad" folk see people with lots of money as dishonest and snake-like. The assumption is that they have stepped on people to get there and they spend money on meaningless crap. Some common expressions from this camp: "more for me is less for you", "making a profit is unethical", "hoarding money is selfish".

People in "money is good" have no problem making and chasing money. Typically because it will bring them self-worth and status in the eyes of friends and family. The mechanic is: if I have "x" amount of money, I will be able to have "y" and "z". Security, a holiday, a house, a new car, proof that I have truly "made it". They want money and it will make them happy when they have enough of it.

Well, let me drop a truth bomb on your ass. Both are bullshit.

Money is a "magnifier".

Money by itself is completely neutral. Obviously. It's a number on a screen, or a face on some metal or paper. The way you interpret it is entirely up to you.

It's tempting to look at people with bulging pockets as nothing short of SELFISH BASTARDS, but hear me out.

All money does is magnify you and your character. If you are an idiot, then a lot of money will just make you a bigger idiot. If you are a miser, then money will make you a tighter miser. If you are generous, then money will make you more generous.

If you say it's evil, it's evil. If it's good, then, to you, it's good.

Case in point...

Look at your average joe lottery winner. All of these silly sausages are in the "money is good" camp (that's why they bought a lottery ticket in the first place). At last, the financial hurdle between them and their dream life - houses, cars, boats, gambling, drugs, and boats - was removed. Predictably, all of them ended up exactly where they were before the winnings: on benefits, bankrupt, in prison, or rehab. Or dead. All money did was magnify their desire for shiny things without working for it.

Summarised very well by one of these self-made victims:

"I only started to think about three things -- drugs, sex and gold. The dealer who introduced me to crack has more of my lottery money than I do"

So what happens if you get your shit together before you rise to fortunes? Well, that's the sweet spot, my friend. These are the real movers and shakers of the world you won't hear about in the media because they're too busy pushing the world forward not backward.

Everyone on this list figured out what change they wanted to make before they set off to get the money for it. They knew what they would do with the money when they get it. The "why?" came first.

Adam Braun - singlehandedly changing the non-profit industry

Brett Wigdortz - founder of TeachFirst. Turns graduates into mentors and role models for children in the toughest schools in the UK

Scott Harrison - founder of charity:water, which brings clean water to the people capitalism forgot.

Madame CJ Walker - created hair products for black women in the early 1900's when black people were legally discriminated against. First self-made female millionaire.

Steve Jobs - turned everyday technology in to art

Oprah Winfrey - champion of people "down on their luck" and inspiration to all African-American men and women

Point is this: money is neither good nor bad. It is entirely up to you what you do with it because it's just a tool. Like a hammer. Do you run around grumbling about how hammers can hurt people do you? No. You put up shelves and hooks and shit.

The news will say that a diet of cocaine and prostitutes is inevitable for rich folk. Not the case. Money has and will continue to be used as a tool for positive change so bring it on.

Idiots with money make good headlines. Leaders with money make change.

What do you want to be? A headline or a change-maker?
