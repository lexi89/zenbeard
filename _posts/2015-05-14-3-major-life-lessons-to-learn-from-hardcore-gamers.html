--- title: 3 Major Life Lessons to Learn From Hardcore Gamers date: 2015-05-14 16:00:54.000000000 +02:00 categories: mind-mastery ---

I've been a hardcore gamer since my early teens.

By hardcore I mean spending the equivalent of a full time job every week exclusively on gaming.

I devoted every spare hour to these guys:
DotA 1 and 2
World of Warcraft
Counterstrike 1.6 and Source
League of Legends

A lineup for a typical evening was a WoW raid, a CSS scrim, and a few casual hours of DotA in the early morning.

At school, I would discuss the previous night’s games with friends.

I would write dissertations on DotA heroes and study patch release notes for loopholes.

Even while working an actual full time job, I would still find ways to get a fix — flaking on weekend plans, lying to friends, sleeping less…

I didn’t go "pro", but I was well on the way.

On January 1st 2014, I can't explain why, I went cold turkey. I uninstalled everything.

I gave logins to friends, destroyed installation CDs, and started life from scratch.

Totally fresh. Level zero. The basics.

I found myself thinking:

How do you decide what you want to do?

Is there more to life than getting a job?

That guy seems so happy. How is he doing it?

After a few weeks, I admitted I had no fucking clue and got help.

I swallowed every piece of self-improvement material I could click on. Happiness, relationships, money, work/life balance, sex, everything...

Today, I’m a walking billboard for life transformation.

In the space of one year I:

  • Moved myself Asia for a job I wanted.
  • Learned to write, code, sell, design, blog, speed-read, market, advertise, and manage finances.
  • Worked with the smartest people on the planet.
  • Stopped eating and drinking crap.
  • Got physically and mentally strong.
  • Met the love of my life.
  • Started an online magazine.

Looking back, I can see that gamers are an interesting breed. They have an intuitive grasp on some very important life skills that I believe every human can aspire to:

 

1. Fail like a gamer.

When a gamer experiences a failure, his response is automatic - try again.

Why? Because there are no consequences to failing.

In fact, a typical gamer knows that failing is as much a part of the game as winning. No failure - no progress.

Yet in "real life", we tend to create consequences where there are none. We turn harmless potential failures into life-or-death situations.

Speaking in public, talking to a girl, and creating your art - three common things that have zero downside, yet we paralyse ourselves before we even start.

If we were all a bit more fearless, like gamers, I think the world would be a better place.

Pro-tip: Try to see failure as part of the game instead of focussing on made-up consequences.

 

2. The only difference is time.

In a game, the only difference between someone who is level 15 and someone who is level 80 is the time they have invested in the game.

Yet in the real world we are very busy comparing our early sketches with someone else’s masterpiece.

We compare our level 15 with someone else’s level 80.

Nobody is born a genius at Warcraft or Counterstrike.

Nobody is born a genius at anything - the key ingredient is time.

Pro-tip: The only difference between you and any “successful” person you can think of is that they showed up. Again and again. Day in, day out.

 

3. Create your style.

In the gaming world, nobody wants to be like someone else.

Everybody has their own setup, build, or modifications. You build your own rig, customise your shortcuts, and play the game your way.

If the game allows it, great. If it doesn’t - you find a way anyway. Name any game and you’ll see hundreds of 3rd party add-ons that lets you customise the game regardless of what's allowed.

Yet offline we do the opposite. The majority are scared of customising and creating the life they want. Instead, choosing something that has been done before. Something safe.

Again, we invent consequences to doing something that hasn’t been done before. We think we’ll be cut off, rejected, and hated.

Yet in reality, we love everything that is different. In fact, we love it so much we buy it.

Pro-tip: play the game YOUR way.

 

Conclusion

It’s very easy to take the black & white position - “people should play less video games and focus on life”.

But it’s difficult (and more rewarding) to respect and use the strengths of both.
